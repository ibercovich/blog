synopsis: Cows, Pigs, Wars, and Witches applies cultural materialist analysis to explain practices that appear irrational or mystical—Hindu cow veneration, Jewish and Muslim pork taboos, Melanesian cargo cults, and European witch trials—by tracing them to concrete ecological, economic, and social conditions. Marvin Harris argues that seemingly bizarre cultural behaviors consistently make practical sense once the material circumstances in which they arose are properly understood.
