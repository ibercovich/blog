synopsis: Bullshit Jobs argues that a large and growing share of modern employment consists of roles that workers themselves recognize as socially pointless, and that this phenomenon persists for structural rather than economic reasons. David Graeber categorizes these jobs into five types—flunkies, goons, duct tapers, box tickers, and taskmasters—and examines the psychological harm inflicted on people compelled to perform meaningless work.
