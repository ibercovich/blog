synopsis: Michael M. Rosen applies three figures from Jewish mythology—golems fashioned from clay to serve communities, dybbuks that possess and corrupt, and maggids that inspire creativity—as frameworks for analyzing competing positions in contemporary AI policy debates. The book argues that these ancient narratives about human-made autonomous beings illuminate the risks and possibilities of artificial intelligence in ways that purely technical or economic frameworks miss.
