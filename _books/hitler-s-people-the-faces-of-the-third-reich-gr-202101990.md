Evans examines the Third Reich through interconnected biographical portraits of its key figures, from Hitler's closest deputies like Goebbels and Himmler to lesser-known enforcers and propagandists who made the regime's crimes possible. By tracing how personal ambition, ideology, and moral failure intersected within this cast of individuals, Evans explores how ordinary society carried out a program of systematic evil.
